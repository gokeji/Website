My personal philosophies

Are you better today than you were yesterday?

I am not perfect, yet I am a perfectionist. Very often I find myself trapped in a search for perfection, be it a perfect solution to a problem, a perfect attire for an occasion, or a perfect way of life. However fruitless these attempts may seem at first sight, I do believe I owe a great deal of my knowledge to this "personality flaw".

I am not perfect, but I keep growing and evolving. I ask myself every day, "Are you better today than you were yesterday?" If I can say yes, I am one step closer to the perfection I seek.

Be not angry that you cannot make others as you wish them to be, since you cannot make yourself as your wish to be.

I consider myself an empathetic person, perhaps even someone who values others over myself. While that means I did have to put in some extra effort in realizing the "self", I am happy to be making constant efforts to step into other people's shoes.

This makes me a great person to work with. While I am still critical, because of the high standards I uphold myself, I try to understand where others are coming from and convey my ideas in a way they can accept. That said, I still most enjoy working with people who also make an effort to understand me.

Learn, to discover how ignorant you are, then learn to appreciate that.

There was a time when I felt wiser whenever I learned something new. Now, that feeling is often coupled with the acknowledgement of how lacking I really am. It puts me in shame to ever assume to be wise, and pushes me to learn more. When I look back, though, it delights me to think that younger Gao would be very proud of me.

I love learning new skills. I sing, play guitar, cook Chinese food, work out, swim, play table tennis, and write songs. But above all, I love learning new languages, because they open me up to new perspectives in life. Besides English, Chinese, and Japanese, programming languages and music are among those languages that I cherish to be learning.

MSN Sifang Messenger Client




During my summer internship at Shanghai Huancai Network Technologies Co., Ltd., the company was working on MSN Sifang messenger, an MSN messenger client for mobile devices, at the request of Microsoft China. I led the construction of the client-end of a web application version, built to run on the MeeGo tablet. It allows friends to share activity, look up nearby friends, check in at locations, chat, and more! It is touch enabled with fluid graphics and a native feel.

Built with HTML5, CSS3, Javascript and jQuery libraries.

Music Notation and Harmonization Tool




This is an intuitive music notation software I developed with Charles Yeh and Evan Li, in the Spring of 2011. Select tools in the toolbar and click on the score sheet to input notes, change the clefs, time signature, and key signature.

Choose among a list of suggested chords and the tool harmonizes your melody line with 3 other voices, complete with voice leading. Click the play button to play back your 4-part tune with MIDI!

Written in Java with Swing for UI display. View the code here!

PandaShares




At the request of The Fund for Community Progress, who wishes to increase transparency in non-profit organizations as well as enhance interorganizational communications, I interviewed clients, and then designed and built PandaShares, with a team of 8 people. It is an online profile for NPOs and NPO umbrella organizations, and it also allows effective file transfer and communications between the members.

Written in HTML5 and Django. Click the image to see the working app!

Artsicle Discovery Game




At the request of Artsicle, I reconceptualized the artsicle discovery game in a team with Eda Celen, Wonmin Lee, and Reese Kuppig. It integrates with the Artsicle API to allow users to discover their taste in artwork by repeatedly selecting their favorite of two artworks presented to them. After a few rounds, suggestions will pop up for similar artworks that the user is interested in.

Written in HTML5 and PHP. View the code here!
